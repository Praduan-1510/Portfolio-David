InsightsTap is a live B2B SaaS platform that productises *intent signals* — helping go-to-market teams run enterprise sales with the speed of an e-commerce engine. I led the front-end development and visual overhaul of the public site, the one real, shipped product in this portfolio.

## The problem

Go-to-market is an abstract domain. Intent signals, the "dark funnel", account-based marketing, AI agents, CRM automation across HubSpot and Salesforce — the value is real, but it's invisible and easy to glaze over. The site had to make a dense, technical product instantly graspable and fast, on a high-contrast brand, without dumbing the ideas down.

## My role

Front-end development and visual overhaul. I built the production marketing site and owned the interface that turns the data story into something people can read, trust, and act on — translating the team's go-to-market concepts into layout, motion, and a component system.

> The brief, in one line: make an invisible, data-heavy product feel as concrete and quick as the e-commerce engines it's compared to.

## What I built

**The proposition.** A hero that states it plainly — *"Run your B2B enterprise like an e-commerce store"* — and frames intent signals as the engine underneath.

**The four-step engine.** The core narrative — capture signals → AI processing → smart automation → scale revenue — built as a legible, scannable sequence instead of a wall of copy.

**A high-contrast system.** A consistent component language — the metrics band, the process diagram, the success-stories carousel, the integrations grid — so dense sections stay calm and readable.

**Built for speed and reach.** Responsive layouts from phone to wide desktop, with performance and legibility passes on the heaviest data sections.

## Outcome

A shipped, live site that frames a complex GTM platform clearly and loads fast — it's the production site at insightstap.com today.
